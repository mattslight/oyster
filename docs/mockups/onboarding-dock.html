Design notes · dock + agent-led

  • The only onboarding UI is the dock pill. No big card, no banner, no modal. Popover appears on click, anchored to the dock.
  • Step 1 is the only manual step for the user. Everything after is the agent doing work — Oyster's surface is the demo.
  • Step 2 is hands-off. User pastes a prompt into their connected agent. Agent calls Oyster's MCP tools. Desktop populates live via SSE. Popover shows the action log for transparency.
  • Step 3 is back in Oyster because the memory store is local and the trust decision belongs to the user (not the agent).
  • Dock lives forever. After setup, it shrinks to a tiny "✓ Set up" pill. Click anytime to re-expand. Can host future progress indicators (sync status, background tasks) without growing new UI.
  • Why this is most Oyster: it doesn't teach "here are 3 setup tasks" — it demonstrates "your agent drives Oyster" through the experience itself. Step 1 is the price of entry; step 2 is the pitch being proven.
  • Failure modes: if step 1's auto-detection breaks, popover has a manual "I've connected it" fallback. If step 2's agent never does anything, dock stays at 1/3 and the popover keeps the prompt visible indefinitely.
  • Trade-off vs accordion: dock is discoverable (pulse + placement) but less in-your-face. Strangers on Reddit might not click it immediately. Balance is: lower friction for users who know what they want, slightly higher miss-rate for users who don't notice the dock.
